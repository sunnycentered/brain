| **Cron.app** (cron.app) | Create and share your working hours timezone map | ✅ Basic | Pro $6/mo |

#### Your Time Zone Strategy:
1. **Set your phone to "Home" time zone** (where you live/originally are) — not the local time where you're currently traveling
2. **Block 2–4 hour overlap windows** with your primary clients' time zones daily
3. **Use Worldtimebuddy's "meeting planner"** — drag a slider to find optimal meeting times across all stakeholders

#### Pro Tip: The "Golden Hours" Rule
If your client is in New York (EST, UTC-5) and you're in Bali (UTC+8):
- Your morning 9 AM = their 8 PM previous day (too late for them)
- Your evening 7 PM = their 6 AM same day (their start of day!)
- **Ideal overlap:** You work 1 PM–7 PM Bali time = they work 2 AM–8 AM New York
- Solution: Set your work hours to 1 PM–9 PM local time in Bali, and negotiate flexible hours with your client

### 4.1 Multi-Currency Banking Setup

The biggest financial mistake nomads make: using their home country bank for everything abroad. You'll lose 2–5% on every transaction in hidden foreign exchange fees.

#### The Nomad Banking Stack:

| **Revolut** | Multi-currency account | Free–$12/mo Metal | Instant cards, crypto trading, stock investing | Backup to Wise. Best when you need metal card perks and fast international transfers. |
| **Local bank account** (destination country) | Standard checking | Varies | Pay rent, utilities in local currency without FX fees | Essential for long-term stays (> 1 month). Open with Wise + passport. |

#### Step-by-Step: Opening a Wise Account

1. Visit wise.com and click "Open an account"
2. Enter your country of residence (your current location or home country)
3. Verify identity with your passport (upload photo + selfie) — approved within minutes
4. Your account is created with unique bank details for 30+ currencies:
   - US: USD routing number + ACH account
   - UK: GBP sort code + account number
   - EUR: EUR IBAN
   - AUD, CAD, SGD, JPY, and more
5. **Order your Wise card** ($7 one-time delivery fee) — arrives in 5–10 business days globally
6. **Use it like a normal debit card** — pays anywhere Visa is accepted, at real exchange rates

> 💰 **Savings example:** A $1,000 transfer from US to Thailand via Chase bank = ~$40 in fees + bad exchange rate (~$35 lost). Same transfer via Wise = **~$5 total**. That's a **$70 saving on one transfer**. Over a year of international payments? $300–$500 saved.

### 4.2 Forex Fee Breakdown and How to Avoid Them

Here's what different methods cost you per transaction:

| Method | Fee | Exchange Rate Markup | Total Cost on $1,000 Transfer |
|--------|-----|---------------------|------------------------------|
| Traditional bank (Chase/Wells Fargo) | $25–$45 flat | 2–5% markup | **$65–$85** |

### 4.3 Tax Residency Basics for Nomads

> ⚠️ **Disclaimer:** This is general information, not legal advice. Always consult a tax professional in your home country and destination countries.

#### Key Concepts:
- **Tax residency** determines which country can tax your worldwide income
- Most countries consider you a resident if you spend > 183 days there per year
- The USA taxes based on citizenship, not residence (Americans abroad still file US taxes)
- Countries with NO income tax for foreigners: UAE, Panama, Costa Rica, Georgia

#### What You Need to Do:

2. Understand "physical presence rules" for each country you visit > 30 days

4. Consider setting up a business entity (LLC, Ltd.) in a nomad-friendly jurisdiction

### 5.2 Public WiFi Threat Models and Countermeasures

#### The Danger:
Every public WiFi network is a potential attack vector. Here's what happens when you connect to an open cafe or hotel network without protection:

- **Man-in-the-middle attacks:** Someone intercepts your traffic
- **DNS hijacking:** You're redirected to fake login pages for banks
- **Packet sniffing:** Someone reads your unencrypted data (passwords, messages)

#### Your Defense Strategy:

| Threat | Countermeasure | How It Works |
|--------|---------------|-------------|
| Man-in-the-middle | VPN encrypts all traffic | Even if someone intercepts it, they can't read anything |
| Fake WiFi networks | Always verify SSID with staff before connecting | Attackers create "FreeWiFi" or "Starbucks_Guest" to impersonate real networks |
| Unencrypted HTTP sites | Use HTTPS Everywhere browser extension | Forces all connections through secure HTTPS |
| DNS hijacking | Use encrypted DNS (Cloudflare 1.1.1.1 or Quad9 9.9.9.9) | Prevents attackers from redirecting your DNS queries |

#### Public WiFi Checklist:
- [ ] Connect via VPN FIRST, before opening any browser
- [ ] Verify the network name with the establishment staff
- [ ] Never access banking apps on public WiFi (use mobile data instead)
- [ ] Disable file sharing in system settings
- [ ] Log out of all accounts when finished

### 5.3 Data Backup Strategy — The 3-2-1 Rule

The **3-2-1 backup rule** is the gold standard:
- **3** copies of your data
- On **2** different storage media types
- With **1** copy offsite (cloud)

#### Implementation for Nomads:

```
Copy 1: Primary files on laptop (working files)
        → SSD with BitLocker/FileVault encryption
  
Copy 2: Cloud sync via Google Drive / Dropbox
        → Automatically syncs when connected to WiFi
        → Works across all devices
  
Copy 3: Encrypted backup on external SSD in your bag
        → pCloud Crypt or Veracrypt encrypted container
        → Manual weekly backup (set a recurring calendar reminder)

```

#### Critical Data That Needs Backups:
- All client files and contracts
- Financial records (invoices, tax documents)
- Personal documents (passport scans, insurance policies)
- Passwords and seed phrases (in 1Password "Secure Notes")
- Photos from your travels (use Google Photos or Dropbox auto-upload)

### 5.6 Emergency Protocols — Lost Phone / Stolen Laptop

#### If Your Phone Is Stolen:
1. **Call your carrier immediately** to suspend SIM (prevents eSIM/account takeover)
2. **Remote wipe via Find My iPhone / Google Find My Device**
3. **Report to local police** (get a report for insurance claims)
4. **Contact Wise/Revolut** to freeze cards
5. **Enable 2FA re-setup** using backup codes stored in 1Password
6. **Order replacement eSIM** via Airalo app on a new device

#### If Your Laptop Is Stolen:
1. **Remote wipe via BitLocker/FileVault** (if enabled)
2. **Report to local police** (get report for insurance)
3. **Cancel your laptop's warranty registration** and update insurance claim
4. **Revoke active sessions** in Google Account, 1Password, GitHub, etc.
5. **Order replacement hardware** — have a "go-bag" plan ready with the exact same specs
