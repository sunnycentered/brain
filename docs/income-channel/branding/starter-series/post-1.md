*🌐 Why I Started @DigitalNomadTech*

Three years ago I was sitting in a 24-hour Starbucks in Chiang Mai, watching the same sunset I'd watched from my apartment kitchen in Austin, and realized: *I had quit a $95K engineering job just to sit in another room with Wi-Fi.*

Not freedom. Not yet. Just geography substitution.

The problem wasn't remote work. It was *income dependency on time*. Every hour I stopped working, the money stopped flowing. That's not location independence — that's remote wage slavery with a better view.

So I did what any paranoid engineer would do: I built systems instead of chasing opportunities.

—

*What I Built (And What Actually Worked)*

1️⃣ **Affiliate content sites** → Generated $30K/year before I killed them. Yes, they work. No, you shouldn't probably build them in 2026 (the game has changed). But the lesson: *automated content + SEO = long-tail income that compounds while you sleep.*

2️⃣ **AI wrapper SaaS tools** → Made $12K/month at peak with a portfolio of 4 small AI-powered micro-tools. Two died after OpenAPI updates. One scaled to $8K/month and I'm still running it. Lesson: *build on your own APIs, not upstream ones you can't control.*

3️⃣ **Automated review blogs** → The highest ROI play in my stack. 17 product reviews indexed by Google, earning $4-7K/month passively with maybe 2 hours of maintenance per month. Lesson: *review content has a longer half-life than tutorials. It's what people search when they're ready to buy.*

—
