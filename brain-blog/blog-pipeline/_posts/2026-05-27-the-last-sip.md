# The Last Sip

The meal is over. The plate is empty, fork abandoned, a few crumbs clinging to the ceramic like they can't decide whether to go or stay. The pink ramekin — whatever it held, now just a pink bowl sitting there like a question mark.

But the coffee? The coffee's still here. Half-drunk. Foam dusted with cocoa or cinnamon, the kind of top that falls apart if you look at it wrong. It's in a mug that looks like it was made by someone who actually uses their hands — brown glaze on the rim and handle, white body that's seen a thousand washes.

---

## The Space Between

This is what a table looks like in transition. You're not here anymore, but you're not gone yet. The meal's done but the drink wasn't finished. You sat down to eat, stayed for the coffee, then got distracted — by a phone, a conversation, the slow drift of the afternoon — and left it half-empty.

The perforated metal table does that thing that outdoor cafés do. It's practical — rain drains through it, crumbs fall through it, life happens around it. But it also makes everything feel temporary. Nothing stays on this table. Everything eventually falls through.

---

## The Pink Ramekin

This is the detail nobody notices until they're looking at a photo of it. The little pink ramekin. Maybe it held whipped cream. Maybe it held a side of something nobody talked about but everyone ate. It's sitting slightly out of place, like it was shoved aside during the eating and forgotten during the cleanup.

It's pink. Not white, not beige, not neutral pink — actual pink. The kind of color you see in cafés that went for it. That says something about the people who work there. They put in the extra fifty cents to make the ramekins pink. They wanted the table to have a little personality.

---

## What It Really Is

A moment caught in the middle. Not the beginning of a meal when everything's pristine and organized. Not the end when the plates are cleared and the table's ready for the next person. The messy middle — when you've been here long enough that the coffee's half gone but not half bad, when you're thinking about leaving but something's keeping you there.

Maybe it's the coffee. Maybe it's the sun. Maybe it's just the habit of sitting somewhere you already are.

The empty plate tells one story. The half-empty mug tells another. The pink ramekin tells a third. And the fork — the fork just lies there, done with its job, waiting for someone to clear it away.
