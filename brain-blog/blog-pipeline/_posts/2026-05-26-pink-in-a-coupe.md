# Pink in a Coupe

The foam head is doing all the heavy lifting here.

It's thick and opaque — a proper egg white foam (or aquafaba, if we're being generous with the word "proper"). The kind of foam that takes a dry shake and a wet shake and a bartender who actually gives a damn about the texture of what they're making. It sits on top of the peachy-pink liquid like a cloud someone built with a cocktail shaker.

---

## The Coupe Was a Decision

Not everyone chooses a coupe. Some places want you in a martini glass because they saw it on TikTok. Some places want you in a rocks glass because that's what's in the drawer. A coupe requires intent — wide bowl, stemmed, the kind of glass that asks the liquid inside to earn its keep.

This one does. The orange-pink color is real, not the kind of neon that looks like it came from a bottle. The bubbles in the foam are tiny — consistent, even. You can feel the shake in them. Whoever made this put their shoulder into it.

The lemon twist on the rim is the only sharp color in the whole frame. Bright yellow against peach foam. It's the highlight someone added after the painting was finished — small, deliberate, the difference between "a drink" and "a drink."

---

## The Rolled Napkin

A rolled napkin on a white plate is the restaurant equivalent of wearing a watch. Nobody needs to see it — it's just there, telling you something about the establishment. The dark marble bar underneath the coupe, the warm dim lighting, the water glass in the background like an afterthought — everything in this frame is saying "we don't need to show off. we've already done it."

The pink drink is the star, sure. But the napkin? The napkin is the whole punchline.

---

## The Shift You're In

This is the kind of drink you order when you're in that weird middle space. Not done with the day. Not started on the evening. You're sitting at a bar, the lighting is warm, the foam is holding strong, and you're trying to figure out if the next part of the day is going to be a conversation, a dance, a long drive, or just more silence with better lighting.

The cocktail doesn't have an answer. It's just sitting there in its coupe glass, pink and foamy and lemon-topped, asking you to decide what happens next.

---

## So Here's What I'll Do

I'll drink this. I'll sit at this bar. I'll watch the foam slowly settle, the lemon twist slowly soften, and the pink slowly give up its structure. And I'll decide what comes next in whatever order feels right.

Because that's kind of the point of this drink. It's not a morning thing. It's not a late-night thing. It's the space between, where everything is still possible.

And honestly, that's what a good cocktail should be. Not the answer to anything. Just the pause before you find out.
