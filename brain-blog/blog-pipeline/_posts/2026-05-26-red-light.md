# Red Light

The color does all the work.

Bright red in a rocks glass. Bold. Unapologetic. The kind of drink that walks into a room and doesn't wait for permission to exist. Thick ice. A straw. That's it. No garnish. No twist. No pretense. Just red, cold, and sitting in a glass that's doing absolutely nothing to soften what's inside.

---

## The Color

Not burgundy. Not pink. Not purple. A bright, vivid red — the kind that makes you stop and think, "wait, what is that?" The color of stop signs. Of fire engines. Of the one shirt you wear when you want to be noticed.

This isn't a color that happens by accident. Someone made a choice. Grenadine? Cranberry? Something else entirely? It doesn't matter. The color is the point.

---

## The Rocks Glass

A proper rocks glass. Thick base. Not delicate. Not trying to be elegant. This is a drink that could be in a martini glass but chose not to be. It's in the kind of glass you'd get if you opened a drawer at a dive bar and pulled out the one glass that survived multiple moves.

It's the perfect vessel for this drink. Neither too fancy nor too cheap. Just a glass that's been there, done that, and still has integrity.

---

## The Straw

A straw in a rocks glass is a practical choice and a confession. It means the drinker didn't want to wait for the ice to melt before getting to the good part. Or it means the drink is strong enough to drink slowly and this is the tool for the job.

Either way, the straw is honest. It's not a garnish. It's a strategy.

---

## The Drink

It's red. It's cold. It's in a rocks glass. What else is there to say? You sip it and it tastes like whatever's inside — whatever someone decided to put in a glass and call a cocktail. The red is the story. The taste is just the detail.

This is a drink that doesn't need to be complicated. It doesn't need a backstory about the bartender's childhood or the origin of the ingredients. It just needs to be red and cold and in front of you.

---

## The Mood

A red drink is a mood. It says "I'm here and I'm not hiding it." It's the cocktail equivalent of showing up in red when everyone else is wearing neutral. It's the visual anchor of the table. It's the first thing you see and the thing you don't forget.

You don't order a red drink when you want to blend in. You order it when you want to be seen.

---

## So Here's What I'll Do

I'll drink it. I'll watch the ice melt and the color slowly lighten. I'll take my time with it because that's what red drinks deserve — not rushed, not analyzed, just consumed slowly while the color does the talking.

The straw is doing its job. The ice is doing its job. The red is doing its job.

I'm just along for the ride.
