# The Wall Said What He Couldn't

There's something almost impossibly tender about a monster holding flowers.

I was walking past this wall when I saw it — this massive, dripping ram skull in neon green, all sharp horns and melting slime, eyes hollow and teeth bared. It looked like something out of a nightmare. A demon painted across an entire building.

And then I looked closer.

In its clawed hand, right against that ridiculous, feral grin, were purple flowers. Soft, round, impossibly delicate. Tucked into a palm made of bone.

**It was the most romantic thing I had ever seen.**

---

## Here's What It Made Me Think About

We have this weird idea of romance. Like it has to look a certain way.

Romance is roses and candlelight. It's chocolates and slow dancing. It's the script we've been handed, and we recite it because that's what we've been told love looks like.

But real romance — the kind that sticks with you — doesn't always follow the script.

Sometimes it's a graffiti artist who spent their Friday night painting a terrifying skull on a wall and then, without fanfare, tucked flowers into its hand.

Sometimes it's the person who remembers how you take your coffee.

Sometimes it's showing up to a friend's door at midnight with soup when they're sick, no big speech, no grand gesture — just *being there*, like a dumb, loyal monster with flowers in its hands.

---

## The Skull Gets It

That wall doesn't care about your aesthetic. It doesn't care about Instagram or being poetic in a way that works for other people. It's big, loud, and a little bit unhinged.

And yet — flowers.

It makes me think about the people in my life who are like that. Who look like a nightmare if you only glance at them but carry something soft underneath. The friend who cracks jokes when everything's falling apart. The partner who doesn't say "I love you" but shows it in a hundred tiny ways. The artist who paints monsters but puts hearts in their hands.

**That's romance.** Not the script. The real thing.

## And Here's My Question For You

What's the most unexpectedly romantic thing you've ever seen?

Maybe it wasn't a grand gesture. Maybe it was a weird, weird little thing that somehow hit you right in the chest.

Tell me below. I want to know.
