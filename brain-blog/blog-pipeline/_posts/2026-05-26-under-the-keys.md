# Under the Keys

No foam. No tricks. Just deep red liquid in a coupe glass, clear and still and completely unapologetic about what it is.

This is the kind of cocktail that arrives without ceremony. No dry shake, no foam head, no citrus garnish trying to be interesting. A cherry on a pick resting across the rim. That's it. The drink is the drink. The cherry is the only garnish it needs.

---

## The Color

Crimson. Not pink, not orange, not the kind of red that looks like it came from a bottle. Deep, rich, almost burgundy. This is a stirred drink — you can tell from the lack of turbulence. This wasn't shaken or blended or muddled. This was built slowly with ice, the way you build a conversation when you have nowhere to be.

The coupe glass is classic — wide bowl, long stem, the kind of glass that was designed when people still sat down and drank things that were named. Not a rocks glass for the "casual" approach. Not a margarita glass for the Instagram approach. A coupe, because someone still believes in the shape of a drink.

---

## The Coaster Tells the Story

"KEYS" in red on the coaster. You sit on a bar and you look down and there it is — the name of the place printed beneath your glass. You can feel the identity of a place from its coasters. A branded coaster means "we exist." A coaster that's worn at the edges means "we've been here a while." The fact that it's paper, not wood or plastic, means they're not trying to be permanent. They're trying to be honest.

The bar behind them is worth mentioning. Rows and rows of bottles — not the curated, five-shelf "we have taste" setup. A real well. The kind of bar where the bartender can make anything because they've already seen what people order.

---

## The Cherry

A cherry on a pick is the cocktail equivalent of a signet ring. Old rules, new drinks. It's not there for flavor — it's there because that's what you do when you're making a proper cocktail. You garnish it the way it's always been garnished, and you don't ask why.

The cherry is dark. Half-submerged in the red liquid. The pick rests across the rim like it belongs there. If you pull it out and eat it, it will be too sweet. That's fine. That's not the point. The point is the ritual.

---

## The Drink Itself

You sip it. It's balanced — not too sweet, not too bitter, not too anything. It tastes like ingredients that were chosen by someone who knows what they're doing. The deep red color comes from whatever it comes from — maybe Campari, maybe amaro, maybe wine. The color is the only thing you can read from the outside. Inside, it's just a drink that does what it's supposed to do.

No foam. No experiment. No "what if we add this." Just a cocktail that has been perfect for a hundred years and doesn't need your permission.

---

## The Night This Belongs To

This isn't a "let's try something new" drink. This is the drink you order when you've stopped trying and started enjoying. When the evening has settled into a rhythm and you want something that doesn't ask questions.

A coupe glass. A cherry. A coaster that says "KEYS." The bottles behind the bar doing what they've always done — sitting there, waiting, being ready for whatever comes next.

Sometimes the best drinks are the ones that stopped trying to be interesting and just became good.
