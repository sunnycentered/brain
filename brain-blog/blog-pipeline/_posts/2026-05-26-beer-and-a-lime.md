# Beer and a Lime

Someone decided a bottle wasn't enough and the world got better for it.

Dark amber beer in a tall glass. Heavy frost on the outside — the kind of condensation that tells you the glass came out of a walk-in and the bartender didn't rush it. A bright green lime wedge on the rim, freshly cut, the only real color in the frame.

It's not fancy. It's not trying to be. It's beer. With a lime. And honestly, that's the whole thesis.

---

## The Lime Wedge

The lime wedge is doing the same job it's always doing. It's not a garnish — garnishes are for people who want to look at their drink before they drink it. The lime wedge is an invitation. "Squeeze me in when it tastes flat." "Squeeze me in when you need more." "Squeeze me in because you can."

It's bright green. Fresh. Sitting on the rim like it owns the place. Which, in a way, it does. The lime is the one thing in this glass that makes a decision about what happens next.

---

## The Beer

Dark amber. Not black. Not golden. Somewhere in the middle — a rich brown that suggests it was brewed by people who take their job seriously but not themselves seriously. The kind of beer you drink because it tastes good, not because it tells a story about the brewery.

No foam. Clean pour. The kind of beer that arrives like a promise kept. Cold. Straight to the point. No drama.

---

## Who First Thought of This?

Beer with lime is one of those combinations that makes you wonder who first thought of it and whether they were a genius or just thirsty. Probably both. Someone opened a bottle, took one sip, and went "this needs more." They found a lime, cut a wedge, put it on the rim, and saved the drink.

That's it. That's the entire history of this drink. Someone was thirsty, someone had a lime, and the combination was so good that everyone else just ran with it.

No committee. No focus group. No brand strategy meeting. Just a person, a beer, and a lime who said "I can fix this."

---

## The Kind of Night This Belongs To

This is not a "let's try something new" drink. This is not a "let's see what the bartender recommends" drink. This is the drink you order when you know what you want and you're not afraid to ask for it.

A beer. A lime. Squeeze it in when it tastes flat. Done.

It's the cocktail equivalent of wearing comfortable shoes to a party. Nobody's impressed. Everyone's relieved. You're the one who brought the right shoes.

---

## So Here's What Happens

You take a sip. The beer is cold and clean and doesn't need anything. You squeeze the lime. The beer is still cold and clean but now it's also got something else — something bright and sharp that makes it feel like you did something.

You didn't do anything. The bartender made the drink. The bartender put the lime on the rim. But you squeezed it, and that matters.

That's the whole point. The lime wasn't there for the beer. It was there for you.
