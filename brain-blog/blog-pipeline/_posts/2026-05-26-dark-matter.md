# Dark Matter

The drink is so dark it might as well be a void.

Rocks glass. Nearly opaque liquid — not quite black, but close enough that you're squinting into it. Some kind of foam or crema sitting on top like the drink has a ceiling and is respecting it. An orange peel twist resting on the rim, oils still glistening, the only bright thing in the entire frame.

---

## The Peel is the Point

It's the only light in the room. Everything else — the dark liquid, the dark bar, the dark background — is in the same color family. The peel is the spotlight. It's bright orange, curled into a spiral, sitting on the rim like a period at the end of a sentence.

If this drink were a person, it would be someone who talks quietly and listens loudly, and the orange peel is the one thing they let you see that's cheerful.

---

## What's in the Glass

It could be anything. Chocolate. Coffee. Stout. Coffee liqueur mixed with something darker. The thickness suggests it's not just poured — it's built. Layers. Maybe shaken, maybe stirred. The kind of drink that requires the bartender to make a decision rather than reaching for a bottle.

The slight foam on top tells you it was handled with care. Not shaken into oblivion, not stirred into nothing. Something in between. The bartender knew what they were doing and did it.

---

## The Bar Knows You

This is the drink that arrives when the bar has sized you up and decided what you actually need. Not what you asked for. What you *need*. When the bartender looks at you and goes "this one's on me" or "try this" without reading the menu, that's when you know. The bar knows.

The wooden bar underneath the glass has knots and grain that have seen a thousand decisions like this one. This isn't a new bar. This is a real one. The kind of place where the bartender has been there long enough to memorize what everyone drinks.

---

## The Shape of This Night

Dark drinks are late-night drinks. They're not for the beginning of the evening. They're not for when you're performing or when you want to feel clever. They're for when you've done all the talking you're going to do and you're ready to sit still.

A bright cocktail at 7pm is energy. A dark cocktail at midnight is resolution.

The orange peel is the only reminder that the world outside still has color.

---

## What's Left

The glass is half gone. The peel has surrendered and is now just floating in the center like a leaf in a pond. The dark liquid has lost some of its opacity as the ice melts and water dilutes whatever was in there. It's becoming something else. Something closer to what it wanted to be all along.

That's the thing about dark drinks. They don't start elegant. They become elegant, slowly, as they warm and soften and admit they're not as serious as they tried to look.
